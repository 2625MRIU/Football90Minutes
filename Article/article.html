The Impossible Dream Realized: Lionel Messi and Argentina Claim Football's Crown in the Greatest Final Ever

Published: Sept 26, 2025 • Author: Football 90 Minutes

Lusail, Qatar – December 18, 2022 In a night of transcendent drama at the Lusail Stadium, a drama so exquisite and agonizing that it rewrote the script of World Cup finals forever, Lionel Messi finally claimed the one trophy that had perpetually eluded him. Argentina defeated France in a penalty shootout after an unprecedented 3-3 draw, achieving not just a World Cup title, but a crowning moment of sporting destiny that had felt preordained by a global audience. This was not merely a football match; it was the ultimate, cathartic final act in a career that has defined a generation. It was the moment the 'Greatest of All Time' debate, for many, was silenced by the roar of history.

The match itself was arguably the most thrilling final in the tournament’s 92-year history, a rollercoaster of emotion that saw Argentina race to a dominant 2-0 lead, only for the defending champions, France, spearheaded by the magnificent Kylian Mbappé, to mount a staggering, late comeback. Messi, playing in a record 26th and final World Cup match, was the architect of Argentina's early dominance. He converted a penalty in the 23rd minute and was central to the stunning team move that allowed Ángel Di María to double the lead in the 36th. The script seemed written, the trophy engraved. But football, especially at this altitude, rarely respects the tidy narrative. Mbappé, the heir apparent to the game's throne, scored twice in quick succession—including a stunning volley—to force extra time. In the ensuing extra periods, the drama intensified. Messi poked home his second goal in the 108th minute, sending a wave of blue and white euphoria through the packed stadium. Yet, Mbappé answered again, completing the first World Cup final hat-trick since 1966 with another penalty, dragging the game to a decisive shootout.

The Weight of a Nation Lifted

The penalty shootout became the crucible where 36 years of Argentine yearning—and Messi's personal quest—was tested one last time. As the noise of the Lusail Stadium reached a fever pitch, it was Argentina’s goalkeeper, Emiliano Martínez, who emerged as the hero of the moment, saving France’s second penalty. Then, defender Gonzalo Montiel stepped up for the deciding kick. When the ball found the back of the net, an entire nation, and millions around the world who had joined their cause, erupted. Messi, the little magician who had carried the weight of expectation for nearly two decades, sank to his knees, finally enveloped not by pressure, but by pure, unadulterated triumph.

The Crown of the 'GOAT'

For Messi, the victory marked the completion of football. He had won everything else—multiple Ballon d'Or awards, Champions League titles, domestic leagues, and the Copa América with Argentina. The World Cup was the final, unassailable piece of his legacy. As he was draped in the traditional Qatari 'Bisht' robe and prepared to lift the golden trophy, the moment was more than a celebration of an athletic achievement; it was the ultimate validation of resilience, a testament to the idea that the greatest prizes demand the greatest patience. "I can't describe the feelings I have right now," an emotional Messi said after the match. "I knew God was going to give it to me. Now I will enjoy it." The image of Lionel Messi, hoisted onto the shoulders of his teammates, finally holding aloft the FIFA World Cup trophy—in his fifth and final attempt—will forever stand as a monument to sporting perseverance and the power of a perfect, albeit wildly improbable, ending. The impossible dream was, at last, realized. The debate is over. Messi has the crown.

The Messi Effect: How One Man Propelled MLS to Global Status

Published: Aug 1, 2025 • Author: Football 90 Minutes

Lionel Messi's arrival at Inter Miami in July 2023 was not just a transfer; it was a seismic event that immediately transformed Major League Soccer (MLS) from a growing regional league into a global football phenomenon. The move injected unprecedented star power, financial muscle, and worldwide visibility into the North American game, fulfilling a long-held ambition for the league. The Unprecedented Popularity Surge
The "Messi Effect" has been quantified across virtually every metric, establishing him as the most significant factor in the league's history for driving popularity and revenue:

Record-Breaking Attendance: Messi's road games turned into must-see events, with oppos ing clubs recording single-match attendance records. The MLS as a whole recorded its h ighest-ever average attendance per match, surpassing 23,200 fans in 2024. In total attendance for the 2024 season, MLS ranked second globally, surpassing Germany's Bundesliga, Spain's La Liga, and Italy's Serie A.
* The Apple TV Revolution: Messi’s signing coincided with the first year of the league's massive 10-year, $2.5 billion broadcasting deal with Apple TV's MLS Season Pass. His debut game alone reportedly led to over 100,000 new subscriptions to the service in a single day, instantly validating the league's pivot to a streaming-first model. Global viewership for Inter Miami games, particularly those involving Messi, routinely draws audiences exceeding 2 million.

Ronaldo’s career is marked by versatility and adaptability. He conquered the Premier League, La Liga, Serie A, and now thrives even outside Europe.
